Advertisement Special Dispatches to the New-York Times.MARCH 19, 1869
The debate on the Tenure-of-Office bill was continued in the Senate to-day, but all the proceedings in reference to it were flat, stale and unprofitable. An exception, however, must be made in favor of Mr. MORTON'S remarks, which occupied ten or fifteen minutes.
